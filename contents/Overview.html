Explanation of Japan's Legislative Structure

  • The House of Representatives (Shuugiin) has 465 seats. Term: 4 years.
  • The House of Councillors (Sangiin) has 248 seats. Half is re-elected every 3 years. Term: 6 years.
  • The House of Representatives holds supremacy in legislation, budget approval (natural passage), and prime minister selection.

Current Power Balance

Ruling Coalition (LDP + Komeito): Not holding a lower house majority (191+24=215 / 233 needed).
Possible Alliance (LDP + Komeito + CDP): Speculative alignment, under public scrutiny.

Major Political Parties (as of June 2025)

Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) - 自由民主党

Leader: Shigeru Ishiba
Defense: Strong stance, promoting pre-emptive capabilities.
Diplomacy: Deep U.S. alliance and regional stability.
Economy: Redistribution + middle-class support. Official site

Komeito - 公明党

Leader: Tetsuo Saito
Defense: Supports alliance, cautious on expansion.
Diplomacy: Advocates China relations and nuclear abolition.
Economy: Humanitarian socialism. Official site

Constitutional Democratic Party (CDP) - 立憲民主党

Leader: Yoshihiko Noda
Defense: Defensive, anti pre-emptive strike.
Diplomacy: Multilateralism, critical of SOFA.
Economy: Welfare, gender equality, decentralization. Official site

Democratic Party for the People (DPFP) - 国民民主党

Leader: Yuichiro Tamaki
Defense: Balanced, moderate enhancement.
Diplomacy: U.S.-Japan alliance, economic diplomacy.
Economy: Growth + realistic reforms. Official site

Japan Innovation Party (Ishin) - 日本維新の会

Leader: Hirofumi Yoshimura
Defense: Constitutional revision, expansion.
Diplomacy: Hardline, aligns with LDP.
Economy: Decentralization, free education. Official site

Japanese Communist Party (JCP) - 日本共産党

Leader: Akira Koike
Defense: Pacifist, anti-armament.
Diplomacy: Neutralism, peace diplomacy.
Economy: Anti-capitalist, pro-worker. Official site
